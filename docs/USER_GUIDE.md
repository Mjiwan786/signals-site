## Overview

The AI-Predicted-Signals dashboard provides real-time access to AI-powered crypto trading signals generated by a sophisticated ML ensemble (LSTM + Transformer + CNN). The dashboard allows you to:

- **View real-time trading signals** with confidence scores and risk parameters
- **Monitor PnL performance** with live equity curves and metrics
- **Analyze backtest results** for historical performance
- **Track signal accuracy** and win rates
- **Access on any device** (desktop, tablet, mobile)

**Key Features:**

- Real-time signal updates via Server-Sent Events (SSE)
- Sub-second signal delivery (<1s from generation to display)
- Comprehensive risk management parameters
- Interactive charts and visualizations
- Graceful degradation when offline

## Understanding Signals

### Signal Quality Indicators

#### 1. Confidence Score (0-100%)

Represents the model's certainty about the signal.

- **90-100%**: Extremely high confidence (rare)
- **75-90%**: High confidence (recommended for trading)
- **60-75%**: Medium confidence (use with caution)
- **<60%**: Low confidence (typically filtered out)

#### 2. Agreement (0-100%)

Measures consensus between the three models (LSTM, Transformer, CNN).

- **90-100%**: All models agree strongly (most reliable)
- **75-90%**: Strong consensus
- **60-75%**: Moderate agreement
- **<60%**: Models disagree (less reliable)

#### 3. Model Regime

The detected market regime affects signal reliability:

- **Trending Up**: Good for LONG signals
- **Trending Down**: Good for SHORT signals
- **Ranging**: Best to avoid or use mean reversion
- **Volatile**: Higher risk, larger stop-loss needed
- **Calm**: Lower volatility, tighter stops possible

### Signal Probabilities

Each signal includes probability distribution:

```
Probabilities:
â€¢ LONG:    75%
â€¢ SHORT:   10%
â€¢ NEUTRAL: 15%
```

This shows the model's confidence across all possible outcomes. A strong signal has high probability for one direction and low probabilities for others.

### Individual Model Predictions

See how each model voted:

```
LSTM:        LONG (80%)
Transformer: LONG (88%)
CNN:         NEUTRAL (70%)
```

**Use this to assess:**

- Consensus (all models agree = more reliable)
- Disagreement (models conflict = less reliable)
- Model-specific biases

## Using Signals for Trading

### Step 1: Review Signal Quality

Before acting on a signal, check:

1. **Confidence**: Is it >70%?
2. **Agreement**: Is it >75%?
3. **Regime**: Does it match the signal direction?
4. **Risk Parameters**: Are they within your risk tolerance?

### Step 2: Apply Risk Management

**Use the provided risk parameters:**

- **Position Size**: Never exceed recommended position size
- **Stop Loss**: Always set a stop-loss at the recommended level
- **Take Profit**: Set take-profit target at recommended level

**Example:**

```
Signal: BTC/USDT LONG
Confidence: 85%
Position Size: 75% of capital
Stop Loss: 2.0%
Take Profit: 4.0%
```

**Action:**

1. Enter LONG position with 75% of available capital
2. Set stop-loss at -2.0% below entry
3. Set take-profit at +4.0% above entry

### Step 3: Monitor Position

- Check PnL dashboard regularly
- Watch for new signals on the same pair (may indicate exit)
- Respect stop-loss and take-profit levels

### Step 4: Record Results

- Track your actual trades vs. signal recommendations
- Compare your performance to platform backtest results
- Adjust risk parameters based on your risk tolerance

## FAQs

### General Questions

**Q: How often are signals generated?**

A: Signals are generated continuously as new market data arrives. Frequency varies by market volatility, typically 5-20 signals per hour across all pairs.

**Q: What is the difference between paper and live trading?**

A: Paper trading uses simulated capital (no real money), while live trading uses real funds. Always test strategies in paper mode first.

**Q: Can I customize the risk parameters?**

A: The platform provides recommended risk parameters. You can adjust them for your trading, but we recommend starting with the defaults.

**Q: How accurate are the signals?**

A: Historical backtests show ~65-70% win rate. Past performance does not guarantee future results. Always use risk management.

### Technical Questions

**Q: Why am I seeing "Metrics unavailable"?**

A: This means the API is temporarily unavailable. The system will automatically retry. Check status at https://crypto-signals-api.fly.dev/health/live

**Q: How do I enable browser notifications?**

A: Click the bell icon in the navigation, then allow notifications when prompted by your browser.

**Q: Can I export signal history?**

A: Yes, use the "Export" button on the signals page to download CSV or JSON format.

**Q: Does the dashboard work offline?**

A: The dashboard requires internet connection for real-time signals. Some cached data may be available offline.

### Trading Questions

**Q: Should I trade every signal?**

A: No. Be selective. Only trade signals that meet your confidence and risk criteria.

**Q: What if models disagree?**

A: Low agreement (<75%) indicates uncertainty. Consider skipping these signals or using smaller position sizes.

**Q: How do I know when to exit a position?**

A: Use the recommended stop-loss and take-profit levels. You may also exit early if a new signal suggests reversal.

**Q: Can I use these signals on any exchange?**

A: Yes, but check if your exchange offers the trading pairs. Signals are generated for major pairs available on most exchanges.
